Sources of Contamination
If water is still, warm, not enclosed, has high pH levels, and frequently contains excess nutrients like nitrogen and phosphorus due to being in close proximity with a farm, finding algae is more likely.
Algae in Water
The contamination of algae in water would result in either a green, red, brown, or blue color; earthy, algae-like odor or no odor; and moldy, earthy taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing algae include stomach pain; vomiting; diarrhea; headache; skin, eye, nose, or throat irritation; and/or neurological symptoms.
Next Steps
Based on the depth and diameter of the well, add a specific amount of chlorine bleach to 11-19 liters of water from the well.
Add it to the well for 12 hours and then turn on all faucets until the chlorine can no longer be smelt in the water. Contact a water treatment professional.

Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to agricultural fertilizers and industrial waste near lakes and reservoirs, finding ammonia is more likely.
Ammonia in Water
The contamination of ammonia in water would result in a yellow color; chemical-like odor; and moldy, earthy taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing ammonia include coughing; chest pain; wheezing; difficulty breathing; fever; dizziness; eye, ear, nose, or throat pain; swelling; nausea; vomiting; confusion; shock; and fainting.
Next Steps
Boiling water can remove free ammonia. The consumption of ammonia does not result in a high number of health risks and is uncommon; therefore expensive filters are unnecessary until the ammonia leads to coliform bacteria where filters are needed for safety. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If lake or reservoir water is in proximity with mining operations and geothermal activity near lakes and reservoirs, finding arsenic is more likely. Arsenic can also leach into groundwater through natural rocks and soil.
Arsenic in Water
The contamination of arsenic in water would result in a bronze color, no distinct odor, and no distinct taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing arsenic include thickening or discoloration of the skin; skin cancer; nausea; vomiting; diarrhea; numbness; depression; headache; tumors in the lungs, bladder, kidneys, and liver.
Next Steps
Arsenic can be removed through ion exchange, membrane filtration, and iron and aluminum coagulation. Water containing arsenic should not be used for drinking or cooking but can be used for bathing and other purposes. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to livestock cesspools, chemical dumps, and farms, finding bacteria is more likely.
Bacteria in Water
The contamination of bacteria in water would result in a cloudy/tinted color; earthy, algae-like odor; and earthy, moldy taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing bacteria include diarrhea, vomiting, fatigue, dizziness, and urinary tract problems.
Next Steps
Bacteria can be removed using a reverse osmosis filter or other filtration methods. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to sediments in runoff or at the bottom of the well, finding clay, silt, or sand is more likely.
Clay, Silt, or Sand in Water
The contamination of clay, silt, or sand in water would result in a brown or cloudy color; earthy, dirt-like odor; and earthy, sandy taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing clay, silt, or sand include diarrhea, vomiting, fatigue, dizziness, and urinary tract problems.
Next Steps
Raising the pump can avoid the sediments on the bottom of the well. Well casing and seals can keep out runoff, and at home, centrifuge systems and filters can be utilized. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is running through corroding copper pipes, finding copper is more likely.
Copper in Water
The contamination of copper in water would result in a green or blue color, metallic odor, and metallic taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing copper include gastrointestinal distress, anemia, and liver and kidney damage.
Next Steps
Replace pipes and contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to runoff from pools, gardens, or chemically cleaned areas, finding disinfectant is more likely.
Disinfectants in Water
The contamination of disinfectants in water would result in a clear or cloudy color, chemical-like odor, and chemical-like taste.
Symptoms
The back of the disinfectant bottle or box explains the symptoms of ingestion.
Next Steps
Use a remove osmosis filter or drain the water until the smell of disinfectants no longer remains. Contact a water treatment professional.

Fluoride in Water

Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to gasoline spills or underground storage tank leaks through runoff, finding gasoline is more likely.
Gasoline/Petrol in Water
The contamination of gasoline in water would result in no distinct difference in color, odor, or taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing gasoline include irritation in the eyes and respiratory tract, headaches, lightheadedness, dizziness, nausea, confusion, and liver damage.
Next Steps
Water containing gasoline should not be used for drinking or cooking and exposure should be minimal while bathing. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to sulfur bacteria in the water heater, plumbing, water softener, or groundwater, finding hydrogen sulfide is more likely.
Hydrogen Sulfide in Water
The contamination of hydrogen sulfide in water would result in a white, black, gray, reddish-brown, red, or brown color and a sulfur-life, rotten odor and taste.
Symptoms
The symptoms of drinking water containing hydrogen sulfide in infants include dehydration and diarrhea. Over time, acclimation to hydrogen sulfide occurs.
Next Steps
Hydrogen sulfide can be removed using a carbon or oxidizing media filtrate. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is running through corroding iron or lead, finding the heavy metals is more likely.
Lead or Iron in Water
The contamination of lead or iron in water would result in a brown, yellow, orange, or red color and a metallic odor and taste.
Symptoms
In children, symptoms of drinking water containing lead include damage to the central and peripheral nervous system, learning disabilities, shorter stature, impaired hearing, impaired function of blood cells, and anemia. In adults, symptoms include cardiovascular disease and damage to the kidneys and reproductive system. Symptoms of ingesting iron are skin infection; wrinkles; eczema; acne; diabetes; hemochromatosis; stomach problems; nausea; and damage to the liver, pancreas, and heart.
Next Steps
Lead or iron can be removed using a filter and can be prevented by replacing plumbing. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to mining operations, industrial wastewater, and groundwater near leaching soil, finding manganese is more likely.
Manganese in Water
The contamination of manganese in water would result in a black or dark brown color, gasoline-like odor, and metallic taste.
Symptoms
Drinking water containing manganese mostly affects infants, the elderly, those who are malnutritioned, and those with liver disease. Particularly, the symptoms of drinking water containing manganese in infants include a change in behavior, lower IQ, speech and memory difficulties, and lack of movement control.
Next Steps
Manganese can be removed using water softening, distillation, reverse osmosis filters, or cation exchange filters. Contact a water treatment professional.
Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to fertilized farms, septic system failures, or compost piles, finding nitrates or nitrites is more likely.
Nitrates or Nitrites in Water
The contamination of nitrates or nitrites in water would result in no distinct color, odor, or taste.
Symptoms
In babies under six months, drinking water containing nitrates or nitrites include methemoglobinemia or “blue baby syndrome”. Over time, ingestion of the contaminant leads to cancer.
Next Steps
Nitrites or Nitrates can be removed using a cation exchange filter, water softening, distillation, or reverse osmosis filters. Contact a water treatment professional.

Radioactive Elements in Water

Sources of Contamination
If water is exposed to large mineral deposits and seawater sprays, finding sodium is more likely.
Sodium in Water
The contamination of sodium in water would result in no distinct color or odor but a salty taste.
Symptoms
Drinking water containing sodium would result in a high salt intake. Those with high blood pressure, cardiovascular disease, heart disease, kidney problems, or low-sodium diets, should avoid ingesting water containing sodium.
Next Steps
Sodium can be removed using an ion exchange or reverse osmosis filter or distillation. Contact a water treatment professional.
